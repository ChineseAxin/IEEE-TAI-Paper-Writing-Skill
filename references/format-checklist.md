# IEEE TAI Format Checklist

## Source And Scope

- Confirm that the user approved use of the skill.
- Confirm Word or LaTeX output and the intended article type.
- Work from a copy of the bundled official template.
- Preserve the official class, styles, margins, columns, typography, and heading hierarchy.
- Keep factual placeholders visible rather than inventing missing information.

## Front Matter

- Use title case rather than all capitals.
- Check author names, IEEE memberships, affiliations, addresses, email addresses, funding, and submission notes.
- Keep the abstract at 150-250 words, in one paragraph, self-contained, and free of citations, footnotes, displayed equations, and undefined abbreviations.
- Keep the impact statement at 150 words or fewer.
- Supply concise keywords and order them alphabetically when practical.

## Main Text

- Make the introduction state the problem, gap, contribution, and paper organization.
- Define abbreviations at first use in the main text even if defined in the abstract.
- Use American English, one space after punctuation, and a serial comma.
- Prefer active voice when it improves clarity.
- Use a leading zero for decimals below one.
- Prefer SI units and avoid dimensionally inconsistent mixed-unit expressions.
- Define every symbol before or immediately after first use.
- Number sections, equations, figures, tables, and references consistently.

## Figures And Tables

- Cite every figure and table in numerical order.
- Place each item near its first substantive discussion.
- Use legible labels at final column width.
- Make captions self-contained and explain the significance of the displayed evidence.
- Use `Fig.` for figure references and captions according to the template.
- Submit or retain separate high-quality figure source files.
- Check grayscale readability and avoid relying only on color to encode meaning.

## Equations And References

- Punctuate equations as parts of sentences.
- Refer to an equation as `(1)` except when a sentence begins with `Equation (1)`.
- Use IEEE numbered citations in square brackets and order references by first citation.
- Verify author names, title, venue, year, volume, issue, pages, DOI, and URL where applicable.
- Do not include uncited references or leave cited works absent from the reference list.

## Final Render

- Compile or render without errors.
- Resolve missing fonts, images, citations, cross-references, and overfull content.
- Inspect title block, two-column flow, section breaks, widows/orphans, equations, captions, tables, and bibliography.
- Compare representative pages with `TAI_template.pdf`.
- Remove template instructional text and sample metadata.
- List all unresolved author-input placeholders in the delivery note.
